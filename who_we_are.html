Biography

Angela is a first generation college graduate and immigrant from the Dominican Republic. She is the co-editor of the 2018 publication, Latinx/a/os in Higher Education: Exploring Identity, Pathways, and Success. Prior to her current position, Angela served Associate Vice Provost for Student Affairs, Dean of Student Life and Interim Chief Diversity Officer at Oregon State University. She also served as Dean of Students at the University of Southern Indiana and has held various positions at the University of Vermont, Lynn University, and Mills College.

Angela actively serves as a national and international consultant and is engaged in national professional organizations.  Her national service includes leadership roles in various organizations including serving as an executive officer on the Executive Committee of the Latino Caucus for the National Conference on Race and Ethnicity in Higher Education (NCORE) and various leadership roles in the Student Affairs Administrators in Higher Education Association, also known as NASPA.  Angela served as the national Vice Chair and Chair for the NASPA Latinx/a/o Knowledge Community, a member of NASPA's Equity and Inclusion Commission, the 2016 National Conference Leadership and Planning Team, and faculty for the NASPA Dungy Leadership Institute (DLI) and Escaleras Latinx/a/o professional institute, which she co-directed in 2018.  She also supported the development of the NASPA Latin American and the Caribbean (NASPA LAC) Division, encompassing 41 countries. 

Angela has received several honors and has been recognized nationally for her work in Student Affairs, Diversity, Equity and Social Justice. Batista was the recipient of the 2017 NASPA Latinx/a/o Knowledge Community (LKC) Service Award and the 2013 NASPA LKC Administrator of the Year Award. In her honor, the Angela Batista Social Justice Award was established by the University of Vermont in 1999 and is awarded to an undergraduate student every year.

Biography

Reese started my career in higher education working at the University of Michigan as a Research Assistant on the NSF ADVANCE Project, examining issues of gender equity and promoting women's advancement in STEM fields. After graduating from college, he pursued a Ph.D. in Sociology concentrating in the areas of sociological theory, gender, and sexuality. His Research and scholarship focuses on interpersonal and institutional discrimination against transgender people and the strategies that transgender people use to navigate the social world. He has taught courses at the University of Vermont, SUNY Albany, Middlebury College, and Dartmouth College. Shortly after completing my Ph.D., transitioning  into Student Affairs and Institutional Diversity work, following his passion for examining and improving the relationship between institutions and individuals.

At Champlain, Reese serves as the #2 for the Division of Student Affairs, Diversity and Inclusion and oversees the areas responsible for co-curricular student learning, identity development, community development, and diversity, equity, and inclusion (Office of Accessibility, Office of Diversity and Inclusion, Student Engagement, Student Support Resources, and the Women's and Gender Center). He Also oversees the College's Bias Response Protocol.

Biography

Bruce's love and appreciation for Burlington is what eventually drew him to the University of Vermont and has continued to keep him in the area ever since.  Upon graduation, he received a commission as a Second Lieutenant in the Army Reserve and served 20 years as an Aviation Officer with the Vermont Army National Guard.  Being a traditional Guard member afforded Bruce the opportunity to lead some of the best and brightest citizen soldiers while simultaneously pursuing a civilian career with the Burlington Police Department.  In 2016, after 21 years of police service, he retired as the Deputy Chief of Police in order to accept the position of Campus Public Safety Director with Champlain College. He inherited a talented group of CPS professionals and is excited to continue collaborating as a team, and with other stakeholders, in order to enhance the safety and wellbeing of all that live at, work for and visit Champlain College.
